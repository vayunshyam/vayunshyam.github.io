Creating an "Internal Open Source"

I led a team of 8 consultants to design a data governance solution for Synopsys to create a centralized code-sharing repository to aid cross-team collaboration and code modularity.

About the client

Synopsys is a leading creator of EDA technology and semiconductor IPs licensing, which is a market primarily dominated by Synopsys and Cadence. The company is primarily built through acquisitions, thorugh which it hosts a large suite of products and licenses to major designers i.e. NVIDIA, Intel.

The challenge

Since the company is primarily built from acquisitions, many teams utilize tech stacks and repositories often completely independent from established company practices. This results in several team "silos" which inhibit cross-team collaboration and new member onboarding. In addition, several code components that are not modularized and could be used in other products are not utilized, which results in an efficiency loss.

The solution

Creating an "InnerSource" repository system through which all projects are discoverable in a centralized portal, along with cross-team contributions, standardized documentation, data pipelines, and relevant governance. The first step of the solution was to artchitect a developer portal via Backstage, to view all project goals and documentation. The second was to create and test a governance team structure, and enable oversight for team managers to enable cross-team edits to repositories. Also developed onboarding and cultural shift practices.
